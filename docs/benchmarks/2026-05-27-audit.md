## A/B Benchmark Results — Infrastructure Blocked

**Status**: A/B benchmark **incomplete** for all 4 target skills in this audit cycle. The decision tree below explains the salvage decision.

### Why A/B was abandoned this cycle

1. **Rate limit incident (07:00 → 11:10 JST)**: All 4 initial subagents hit "You've hit your limit · resets 11:10am" with each having only made 2-3 tool uses. Burned ~4 hours of the audit window.
2. **Subagent watchdog stall on retry**: After limit reset, 4 retry subagents stalled at 600s "no progress" — likely because nested `claude -p` blocks the parent stream while waiting on long children. The watchdog can't distinguish a busy-but-progressing child from a stuck one.
3. **Stream idle timeout on direct Bash**: Running `run_eval.sh` directly in background succeeded in starting all 6 `claude -p` children, but 2/6 hung indefinitely with empty responses, 1/6 returned "API Error: Stream idle timeout - partial response received", and only 3/6 produced non-empty outputs. Only **1 complete with/without pair** (`domain-depth`) was usable.
4. **compare.sh silent failure**: `compare.sh` on the 1 complete pair produced no `grading.json` and no error log within 240s timeout.

## Infrastructure & Methodology Notes

- **Subagent driver fragility**: When subagents spawn long-running `claude -p` processes via Bash, the stream watchdog interprets the lack of subagent-level output (vs. child process output) as a stall at 600 seconds. Direct background Bash from the parent session works; subagent delegation does not. **Recommend** updating skill-audit SKILL.md to use direct Bash + Monitor pattern for long A/B runs, or shorten budget via 2-eval mode for subagent reuse.
- **Rate limit hit at 07:00 JST** (resets 11:10) — full 4-hour stall consumed the audit window. Future audits should check `claude -p` headroom before spawning bulk benchmarks.
